Participant Information Sheet

Project title: Improving static websites by eliciting samples from people
Principal investigator: Dr Christopher Lucas
Researcher collecting data: Zike Li

This study was certified according to the Informatics Research Ethics Process, RT number XXXXX [edit accordingly]. Please take time to read the following information carefully. You should keep this page for your records.
Who are the researchers?
Zike Li and Dr Christopher Lucas
What is the purpose of the study?
To apply the “Gibbs sampling with people” method to explore people’s preferences for web parameters of static websites.
Why have I been asked to take part?
Anyone over the age of 18, with normal sensory abilities and from/residing in the UK can be invited.
Do I have to take part?
The answer is ‘No’: It should be clear that taking part is entirely voluntary. The participant can withdraw if he/she later changes his/her mind, without giving a reason. Your rights will not be affected. If you wish to withdraw, we will stop using your data in any publications or reports submitted after you have withdrawn your consent.
What will happen to me if I decide to take part?
You will be asked to move a slider to control the values of different web page parameters to obtain the optimal page readability. The session will last 20 iterations (about 20 minutes).
Are there any possible disadvantages or risks from taking part?
There are no significant risks associated with participation.
Are there any benefits associated with taking part?
There are no direct benefits.
What happens at the end of the study?
The results of this study may be summarised in published articles, reports and presentations. Quotes or key findings will be anonymized: We will remove any information that could, in our assessment, allow anyone to identify you. With your consent, information can also be used for future research.
Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?
The data will be processed in accordance with Data Protection Law. All information collected about you will be kept strictly confidential. Each participant will be assigned to a unique identifier – participants will not be asked for name or any other personal information. Your data will only be viewed by researcher Zike Li and supervisor Dr Christopher Lucas. All electronic data will be stored in a database on a password-protected, encrypted computer. And store more than one copy, the copy could be uploaded to the University’s secure encrypted cloud storage services ownCloud.
What will happen to my data?
The University of Edinburgh is the data controller of the information you provide. You have the right to access the information held about you. Your right of access may be exercised in accordance with the Data Protection Act. You also have other rights, including the right to correct, delete and object. For general information about how we use your data, go to: edin.ac/privacy-research.
Who can I contact?
If you have any further questions or wish to make a complaint about the study, please contact the researcher Zike Li at s2193419@ed.ac.uk. When you contact us, please provide the study title and question/complaint detail.
Updated information.
If the research project changes in any way, an updated Participant Information Sheet will be made available.
